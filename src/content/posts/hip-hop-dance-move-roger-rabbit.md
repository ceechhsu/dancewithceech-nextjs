## Introduction

The Roger Rabbit is a fun and energetic hip hop dance move that became a cultural phenomenon in the late 1980s. Inspired by the floppy movements of the cartoon character Roger Rabbit from the 1988 film "Who Framed Roger Rabbit," this dance craze swept across the nation, capturing the hearts of both hip hop enthusiasts and mainstream audiences alike. With its unique blend of quirky and playful movements, the Roger Rabbit dance move has cemented its place as an iconic part of hip hop culture.

## Dance History of The Roger Rabbit

The Roger Rabbit dance move emerged in the late 1980s, directly influenced by the release of the live-action/animated film "Who Framed Roger Rabbit" in 1988. The movie's lead character, Roger Rabbit, a zany and mischievous cartoon rabbit, captivated audiences with his exaggerated and floppy movements. Inspired by these animated antics, dancers began to imitate Roger Rabbit's signature moves, giving birth to the Roger Rabbit dance craze. While the exact creator of the Roger Rabbit dance move remains unknown, it quickly gained popularity among hip hop dancers and became a staple in social dance circles. The dance's quirky and humorous nature resonated with the playful spirit of hip hop culture, making it an instant hit. ([Hip Hop Dance History](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_hip_hop_dance))

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent.

2. Bend your knees and hips, leaning your upper body forward while keeping your back straight.

3. Extend your arms out in front of you, with your hands hanging loosely at the wrists, mimicking the floppy movements of Roger Rabbit.

4. Bounce up and down, allowing your arms and body to move in an exaggerated and loose manner, as if you're a rag doll.

5. Incorporate kicks, spins, and other playful movements to add variety and personality to your Roger Rabbit dance.

6. Don't forget to exaggerate your facial expressions, channeling the mischievous and zany spirit of Roger Rabbit himself.

## Tips For Learning The Roger Rabbit

- Watch clips from "Who Framed Roger Rabbit" to study the character's movements and mannerisms.

- Loosen up your body and don't be afraid to look silly – the Roger Rabbit is all about embracing your inner goofball.

- Practice the arm and body movements separately before putting them together.

- Incorporate the Roger Rabbit into your freestyle sessions to get comfortable with the dance's flow and energy.

- Have fun with it! The Roger Rabbit is a lighthearted and playful dance move, so don't take it too seriously.
