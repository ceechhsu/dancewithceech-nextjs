## Introduction

The Running Man is a simple yet energetic dance move that mimics the motion of running in place. It involves alternating the movement of the legs, lifting the knees high while simultaneously swinging the arms in a running motion. The dance move is characterized by its exaggerated and stylized movements, making it a fun and entertaining addition to any hip hop routine.

## Dance History of The Running Man

While the exact origins of the Running Man are unclear, it is widely believed to have emerged from the hip hop dance crews in New Jersey in the late 1980s. The dance move gained mainstream recognition after being featured in several music videos and performances by popular hip hop artists of the time, such as MC Hammer and Bobby Brown.

One of the earliest known appearances of the Running Man was in the 1988 music video for "Run's House" by Run-D.M.C. The dance move quickly became a craze, with people of all ages attempting to master its distinctive style.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent.

2. Lift your right knee up towards your chest while simultaneously swinging your right arm forward and your left arm back, as if running in place.

3. Quickly switch legs, lifting your left knee up and swinging your left arm forward and your right arm back.

4. Repeat the alternating leg and arm movements, maintaining a consistent rhythm and exaggerating the movements for added flair.

5. Incorporate variations by adding arm movements, spins, or combining the Running Man with other hip hop dance moves.

## Tips For Learning The Running Man

- Start slowly and focus on the basic movements before increasing the speed and intensity.

- Practice in front of a mirror to ensure proper form and synchronization of the arm and leg movements.

- Incorporate the Running Man into a dance routine or freestyle session to build confidence and fluidity.

- Have fun with it! The Running Man is all about expressing energy and enjoying the rhythm of the music.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the Running Man dance?
The Running Man is a late-1980s hip hop dance move that mimics the motion of running in place — alternating high-knee lifts with opposing arm pumps, on the balls of the feet. It became a mainstream phenomenon through MC Hammer, Bobby Brown, and Janet Jackson's "Rhythm Nation" video, and remains one of the most recognizable dance moves from the era.

### Where did the Running Man dance originate?
The Running Man emerged from hip hop dance crews in New Jersey in the late 1980s. One of its earliest high-profile appearances was in the 1988 music video for "Run's House" by Run-D.M.C. From there it spread rapidly through MTV, music videos, and party culture, becoming a defining move of the late-80s/early-90s golden era.

### Who made the Running Man famous?
MC Hammer and Janet Jackson (in the "Rhythm Nation" video) were the biggest mainstream amplifiers of the Running Man. Bobby Brown, Milli Vanilli, and Vanilla Ice also performed it, and Paula Abdul's choreography featured it. The dance was so widespread in 1989-1991 that it became synonymous with the sound of late-80s hip hop and new jack swing.

### How long does it take to learn the Running Man?
Most beginners can nail the basic alternating step in 10-15 minutes. What takes practice is making it look fluid — the illusion of "running" depends on the back leg sliding BACK the instant the front knee lifts, not just lifting one leg at a time. Expect 1-2 weeks of daily practice to get it crisp.

### What's the most common Running Man mistake?
Lifting the knees without sliding the back foot. The Running Man illusion depends on BOTH feet moving at the same time — the back foot glides backward while the front knee lifts up. Beginners who just lift-step-lift-step lose the "running" effect entirely. Slide and lift on the same beat.
