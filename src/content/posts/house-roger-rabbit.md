## Step By Step Instruction

1. Relax and stand with feet shoulder-width apart, keeping your knees soft.

2. Begin by stepping backward with your right foot, slightly hopping onto it as you move.

3. Allow your left foot to follow loosely behind, almost hovering above the ground.

4. Swing your arms in a relaxed, oppositional rhythm to your legs for balance.

5. Shift the weight to your left foot, stepping backward with a slight hop as well.

6. Allow your right foot to follow in a loose drag, maintaining the casual bounce.

7. Alternate these steps continuously, refining the flow and effortless "hop-back" motion.

## Tips For Learning The House Roger Rabbit

- Focus on the feeling of backward momentum with a smooth, hopping motion.

- Keep the trailing leg relaxed and close to the floor for an authentic glide.

- Practice in front of a mirror to ensure your body looks relaxed and rhythmic.

- Let your arms counterbalance naturally but avoid stiff or exaggerated movements.

- Listen to the music and find the groove that complements the buoyancy of this move.
