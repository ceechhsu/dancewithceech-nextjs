## Dance History of The Salsa Step

The Salsa Step finds its origins in the cross-pollination of Latin dance and the street styles of Hip Hop and House. Salsa, traditionally a partner dance characterized by its lively steps and fluid hip movements, has influenced numerous dance forms with its irresistible energy. As dancers from diverse backgrounds began to mingle in clubs and street dance spaces, the Salsa Step evolved into a solo variation admired in Hip Hop House dance, adopting the foundational steps of Salsa yet executed with the individualistic attitude and flair of urban dance styles.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Begin with feet positioned together and weight balanced equally.

2. Advance left foot forward, shifting body weight to that side.

3. Execute a light tap using right foot beside the left (no weight transfer).

4. Move right foot backward, returning weight to the right leg.

5. Tap left foot beside the right while maintaining right-side weight.

6. Continue repeating this pattern rhythmically, capturing the spirited character of Salsa within Hip Hop House style.

## Tips For Learning The Salsa Step

- Maintain an upright posture to stay agile and responsive.

- Infuse energy into your hip movements, letting them sway naturally.

- Keep the tapping light and quick to maintain the Salsa rhythm.

- Start slow and build up speed as your comfort with the pattern grows.

- Use your upper body to add expressive styling to the step, letting your arms flow loosely.
