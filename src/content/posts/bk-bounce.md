## The BK Bounce Dance Move by Buddha Stretch

The BK Bounce is not merely a step sequence but a groove that embodies the Brooklyn attitude and style. Crafted by the legendary hip-hop innovator Buddha Stretch, this move encapsulates the energetic essence of New York's bustling streets and the spirited pulse of the hip-hop revolution. Buddha Stretch, a pivotal figure in the dance world, gifted the scene with this dance that captures both the smoothness and assertiveness of Brooklyn's vibe. Let's step into the dynamic world of the BK Bounce and learn about its rich heritage.

## Dance History of The BK Bounce

Buddha Stretch is a prominent name in hip-hop dance, known for his work with artists like Michael Jackson and as a member of the Elite Force Crew. He pioneered the fusion of hip-hop with traditional dance styles and was instrumental in bringing street dance into the mainstream. The BK Bounce was born out of the underground dance scene in Brooklyn and was popularized in the 1990s. It reflects the raw, unfettered joy and the community spirit of the hip-hop dance culture.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Starting Position: Find your footing with legs shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent for good bounce potential.

2. The Bounce: Start with a simple bounce motion, dipping your knees to the rhythm, getting comfortable with the beat.

3. The Step: With each bounce, alternate stepping out to the side—first right, then left—with the corresponding hip tilting upwards slightly.

4. Arm Coordination: As you step out, let your opposite arm swing naturally. If you step out with your right foot, your left arm should swing out, and vice versa.

5. Enhance the Bounce: Add more energy to your step-outs and bounces, incorporating the vibrant Brooklyn vibe into each movement.

## Tips For Learning The BK Bounce

- Always start with the bounce—get comfortable with this foundational movement before stepping out.

- As you get more comfortable, allow your body to loosen up and find the natural flow of the bounce and swing.

- Practice to a variety of hip-hop tracks to understand how the BK Bounce can adapt to different tempos.

- Keep working on the coordination between your arms and legs; the fluidity comes with time and repetition.
