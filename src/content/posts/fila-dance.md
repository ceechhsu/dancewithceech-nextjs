## Dance History of The Fila Dance
The Fila dance move, while contemporary in its popularity, draws from a lineage of Hip Hop culture that champions innovation, rhythm, and community. Named after the iconic sportswear brand that gained significant traction in hip hop and urban fashion, the Fila move mirrors the brand's ethos of standing out and being distinctive. Coming into prominence in the late 20th century, this move captures the essence of Hip Hop's ability to repurpose elements from its surroundings into vibrant expressions of art and identity.

 

Though not attached to a single creator, the Fila move's evolution is a testament to the communal development of Hip Hop dance, with dancers from different backgrounds adding their flair and interpretation. It reflects the ethos of Hip Hop – blending style, music, and movement to create something entirely new and exciting.
## Step By Step Instruction

1. Starting Position: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, body relaxed and ready to move.

2. The Stomp: Begin by stomping your right foot firmly on the ground.

3. The Hop: Follow the stomp with a slight hop, shifting your weight onto your right foot as you lightly kick your left foot backward.

4. The Switch: After the hop, bring your left foot back down and repeat the stomp, this time with your left foot.

5. The Turn: Incorporate a smooth, quarter-turn to the right, repeating the stomp-hops with rhythm and flair.

6. Repeat: Continue the sequence, adding in your style, such as arm movements or additional turns, to personalize your ATL Stomp.

## Tips For Learning The Fila Dance

- Start slow to master the footwork before adding speed.

- Focus on fluidity and continuous motion to maintain the groove.

- Use hip hop tracks with a clear beat to practice and find your rhythm.

- Experiment with arm movements – the Fila is about expressing your unique style.

- Practice in front of a mirror to refine your technique and monitor progress.
