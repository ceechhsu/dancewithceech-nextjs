## Introduction

Toyman is a captivating and playful dance move within the funk style family, specifically associated with popping. This unique technique transforms dancers into living, moving toys, creating an illusion of mechanical, jerky movements reminiscent of wind-up figurines. Toyman adds a whimsical and eye-catching element to popping routines, allowing dancers to showcase their creativity and control. By incorporating sharp, precise movements with sudden stops and starts, performers can create a mesmerizing visual effect that captivates audiences and fellow dancers alike. Mastering the Toyman requires a combination of body control, rhythm, and imagination, making it a challenging yet rewarding move for funk style enthusiasts.

## Dance History

The Toyman move originated as part of the broader popping dance style, which emerged in the 1960s and 1970s in California. While the exact creator of the Toyman is not explicitly mentioned in the search results, it is closely associated with pioneering popping dancers like Pop'in Pete and Skeeter Rabbit of the Electric Boogaloos. These influential dancers helped popularize and demonstrate the Toyman style, contributing to its recognition within the funk dance community. The move gained prominence as part of the "old school" popping techniques, becoming a staple in the repertoire of skilled funk style dancers.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Start in a neutral standing position with your arms relaxed at your sides.
2. Tense your muscles to create a rigid, toy-like posture.
3. Bend your elbows sharply, bringing your forearms up parallel to the ground.
4. Move your arms in short, jerky motions, mimicking a wind-up toy.
5. Incorporate sudden stops and starts in your arm movements.
6. Add subtle body movements, such as slight tilts or rotations, to enhance the mechanical effect.
7. Combine arm movements with leg and foot motions for a full-body Toyman effect.
8. Practice transitioning smoothly between Toyman and other popping moves.

## Tips For Learning

- Start by mastering basic popping techniques before attempting Toyman.
- Practice isolating different body parts to achieve precise, controlled movements.
- Use a mirror to observe and refine your Toyman technique.
- Incorporate music with a strong beat to help time your movements.
- Study videos of experienced dancers performing Toyman for inspiration and guidance.
- Combine Toyman with other funk style moves like TwistoFlex for added variety.
- Focus on creating sharp, distinct angles with your arms and body.
