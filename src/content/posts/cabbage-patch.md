## Dance History of The Cabbage Patch Dance

The Cabbage Patch emerged during the golden age of hip hop in the 1980s, named after the then-popular Cabbage Patch Kids dolls. Although the creator of this iconic move is not definitively known, it quickly became a staple in pop culture, often breaking out during celebrations and prominently featuring in music videos and dance routines. The universal appeal of the Cabbage Patch lies in its simplicity and the joyous sentiment it evokes.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Begin with your feet shoulder-width apart, maintaining a slight bend in your knees.

2. Extend your arms out to the side of your body, bent at the elbows, with hands closed in fists.

3. Swing your arms in outward circular motions. Imagine drawing big circles in the air with your fists.

4. While your arms are circling, start swaying your hips from side to side in rhythm with the music.

5. Keep your movements relaxed and let your whole body get into the groove.

## Tips For Learning The Cabbage Patch

- The key to nailing the Cabbage Patch is to relax and enjoy the process.

- Focus on feeling the rhythm and let your movements be guided by the beat.

- Start off slow, mastering the arm circles before adding in the hip sway.

- Once comfortable, speed up and add energy to your movements.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the Cabbage Patch dance?
The Cabbage Patch is an 80s hip hop dance move where both arms rotate in large outward circles in front of the body while the hips sway side to side in rhythm. It's named after the Cabbage Patch Kids dolls that were a pop-culture phenomenon in the same era, and became one of the most recognizable party moves of the golden-age hip hop decade.

### Where did the Cabbage Patch dance come from?
The Cabbage Patch emerged in the mid-1980s during the golden age of hip hop, when party dances named after pop culture (Cabbage Patch Kids, Roger Rabbit, Smurfs, Bart Simpson) dominated music videos and block parties. The exact creator is unknown — the dance spread organically through clubs, MTV, and social dance circles rather than being attributed to a single dancer or crew.

### When was the Cabbage Patch dance popular?
The Cabbage Patch peaked in popularity from 1986-1990, coinciding with the Cabbage Patch Kids toy craze. It appeared in countless music videos, was a staple at school dances and weddings, and remains one of the most recognizable 80s party moves today. It enjoys regular revivals whenever 80s nostalgia resurfaces in pop culture.

### How long does it take to learn the Cabbage Patch?
Most beginners can nail the basic arm-circle and hip-sway in 5-10 minutes — it's one of the easier dance moves to pick up. What separates a good Cabbage Patch from a great one is keeping both arms perfectly synchronized AND matching the hip sway to the beat. That polish takes 30-60 minutes to feel locked in.

### What's the most common Cabbage Patch mistake?
Swinging the arms too fast. The Cabbage Patch looks best with slow, deliberate, confident arm circles that match the BEAT of the music — not the frantic pace beginners default to. Slow down, commit to each circle, and let your hips sway in time. The move is about groove and swagger, not speed.
