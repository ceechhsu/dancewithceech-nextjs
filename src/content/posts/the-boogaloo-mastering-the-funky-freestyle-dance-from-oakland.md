## Introduction

Drawing inspiration from diverse sources such as James Brown, the Temptations, Charlie Chaplin, cartoon characters, and sci-fi robots, Boogaloo dancers created a unique repertoire of moves that captured the essence of the funk era. This dance form, which predates hip hop, became the foundation for other funk styles like strutting and robotting, collectively known as "the funk styles."

## Dance History

The Boogaloo dance emerged in Oakland, California, during the late 1960s and early 1970s. It was created by African American youth who were inspired by various cultural influences, including popular musicians, comedians, and even cartoon characters. The dance style developed alongside the funk music genre, which provided the perfect soundtrack for its energetic and rhythmic movements.

Boogaloo's influence spread to nearby cities, with San Francisco developing a variant called strutting and Richmond creating robotting. These styles, along with Boogaloo, became collectively known as "the funk styles" due to their development during the funk era. The dance form predates hip hop and has had a significant impact on subsequent street dance styles.

The Electronic Boogaloo Lockers formed in Fresno, California, made a name for themselves as well. They later became the Electric Boogaloos and became world famous when they were showcases on Soul Train in 1979.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Start with the basic Boogaloo stance: feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent.
2. Initiate the movement from your hips and legs, creating a rolling motion.
3. Bend your knees rhythmically, following the music's beat.
4. Move your legs in a circular motion, as if you're "going around."
5. Practice the movement going forward and backward (reverse).
6. Incorporate upper body movements, such as arm gestures or head rolls, to complement the leg work.
7. Experiment with different levels by bending lower or rising up on your toes.
8. Add signature Boogaloo moves like the "Dime Stop," "Hit," or "Mack Pose" to your routine.

## Tips For Learning

- Focus on mastering the leg and hip movements before adding upper body gestures.
- Practice on the balls of your feet for better balance and fluidity.
- Study the original Boogaloo dancers and their techniques for inspiration.
- Incorporate Boogaloo elements into ground moves for versatility.
- Listen to funk music to internalize the rhythm and feel of the dance.
- Join local Boogaloo or funk style dance communities to learn from experienced dancers.

## Conclusion

Boogaloo is more than just a dance; it's a cultural expression deeply rooted in African American history and the funk era. By learning and practicing Boogaloo, dancers not only master a unique and dynamic style but also connect with an important piece of dance history. As the original Boogaloo dancers pass their knowledge to new generations, they ensure that this influential dance form continues to thrive and inspire, bridging the gap between classic funk styles and modern street dance.
