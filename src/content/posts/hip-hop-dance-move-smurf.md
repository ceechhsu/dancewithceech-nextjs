## Introduction

The Smurf, also known as "Doing the Smurf," is a fun and simple hip hop dance move that became a craze in the 80s and 90s. It gets its name from the resemblance of the arm movements to the little blue Smurf characters from the Belgian comic and cartoon series. Despite its playful name, the Smurf is a legitimate hip hop dance move that requires coordination and rhythm.

## Dance History of The Smurf

The Smurf dance move is believed to have evolved from an earlier 1960s dance called "The Frug," which was featured in an episode of "The Andy Griffith Show." The Frug involved similar bouncing and arm movements but was considered quite provocative at the time.

In the 1980s, the dance resurfaced in a variation that became known as "The Smurf" due to its popularity coinciding with the Smurfs cartoon series.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Start with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees bent.

2. Bounce up and down on the balls of your feet, keeping your knees bent.

3. As you bounce, swing one arm out in a circular motion, keeping it straight.

4. On the next bounce, swing the other arm out in a circular motion.

5. Continue alternating the arm swings in time with the bouncing motion.

## Tips For Learning The Smurf

- Keep your arms straight and swing them out from the shoulders.

- Bend your knees deeply on each bounce for maximum bounce height.

- Practice the arm and leg movements separately at first, then put them together.

- Listen to the beat of hip hop music to help you stay on rhythm.

## Conclusion

The Smurf is a classic hip hop dance move that has stood the test of time. Its simple yet energetic movements make it a fun addition to any dance routine or party. While it may have a whimsical name, the Smurf requires coordination and groove to pull off correctly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the Smurf dance?
The Smurf is a hip hop dance move built on a steady bounce with alternating circular arm swings — one arm swings out in a wide circle while you bounce, then the other arm takes its turn. Named after the Belgian cartoon characters because the arm motion resembled their animated movement, it became a mainstream dance craze in the 1980s.

### When was the Smurf dance popular?
The Smurf peaked in popularity in the mid-1980s, coinciding with the Smurfs cartoon series that aired on NBC from 1981 to 1989. It appeared in music videos, TV shows, and was a staple at school dances and block parties throughout the decade. It remains a recognizable retro move today.

### Where did the Smurf dance come from?
The Smurf evolved from earlier 1960s dances like "The Frug," which featured similar bouncing and arm movements. The 1980s version was rebranded and popularized in hip hop social dance circles, getting its name from the Smurfs cartoon that was dominating TV at the time.

### How long does it take to learn the Smurf?
Most beginners can learn the basic bounce and alternating arm swing in 10–15 minutes. Getting the timing exactly right — so the arm swings hit on the bounce, not between bounces — takes a bit more practice, usually 30–60 minutes to feel locked in.

### What's the most common mistake with the Smurf dance?
Bending the arms. The Smurf works when the arms stay fully extended and swing from the shoulders in a big, confident circle. Beginners often bend at the elbow, which kills the visual effect. Keep the arms straight and let the shoulders drive the motion.
