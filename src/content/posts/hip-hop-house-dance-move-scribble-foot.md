## Introduction

The Scribble Foot is a dynamic and intricate hip hop dance move that has become a staple in the world of street dance. This move involves a series of quick and precise footwork patterns, creating a visually striking and rhythmic effect. It is a testament to the creativity and skill of hip hop dancers, showcasing their ability to blend technical precision with artistic expression.

## Dance History of The Scribble Foot

The origins of the Scribble Foot can be traced back to the early days of hip hop culture in the 1970s. As breaking and other street dance styles emerged from the streets of New York City, dancers began experimenting with intricate footwork patterns to showcase their skills and individuality. While the exact creator of the Scribble Foot is unknown, it is believed to have evolved from the vibrant street dance scene of that era.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Start in a standing position with your feet shoulder-width apart.

2. Shift your weight onto your right foot and lift your left foot off the ground.

3. Using your left foot, rapidly draw intricate patterns on the floor, as if you're scribbling with a pen.

4. Alternate between drawing patterns with the ball of your foot and the heel, creating a rhythmic and dynamic movement.

5. Incorporate variations by changing the direction, speed, and complexity of the patterns.

6. Repeat the same process with your right foot, maintaining a smooth transition between feet.

## Tips For Learning The Scribble Foot

- Start slowly and focus on control and precision before increasing speed.

- Practice the footwork patterns separately for each foot until you feel comfortable.

- Use a mirror to ensure proper form and alignment.

- Incorporate arm movements and upper body isolations to add style and flair.

- Experiment with different rhythms and music genres to challenge yourself.
