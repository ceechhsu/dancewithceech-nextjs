## Introduction

The Lock is the quintessential move that gave birth to the electrifying dance style known as Locking. Originating in the late 1960s, this foundational move involves freezing from a fast movement and "locking" into a stationary position, creating a striking visual contrast. The Lock is the cornerstone of Locking, a funk-inspired dance form that seamlessly blends rhythmic movements with theatrical elements. Its unique style and infectious energy have made Locking a beloved dance genre worldwide.

## Dance History

The Lock was created by Don Campbell, a young art student in the late 1960s. While attempting to perform the popular "Funky Chicken" dance, Campbell found himself stuck in an awkward position, unable to remember the next step. Instead of giving up, he embraced the freeze and incorporated it into his routine. This accidental move, dubbed "The Lock," quickly gained popularity among his peers and became the foundation of a new dance style, later known as Locking.

## Step By Step Instruction

1. Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent.
2. Raise your arms to chest level, with your elbows bent and your hands facing inward.
3. Initiate a fast, rhythmic movement with your arms, as if you're conducting an orchestra.
4. Abruptly freeze your arms in a specific position, locking them in place while maintaining the rest of your body's momentum.
5. Hold the locked position for a brief moment, accentuating the contrast between the frozen arms and the moving body.
6. Release the lock and continue with the next movement or transition smoothly into another lock.

## Tips For Learning

- Practice the lock in front of a mirror to ensure proper form and timing.
- Experiment with different arm positions and angles to add variety to your locks.
- Incorporate facial expressions and body language to enhance the theatrical aspect of Locking.
- Start with slower tempos and gradually increase the speed as you become more comfortable with the lock.
- Attend Locking workshops or classes to receive guidance from experienced instructors.

## Conclusion

The Lock is the cornerstone of the Locking dance style, a testament to the creativity and innovation of its founder, Don Campbell. By embracing an accidental freeze and transforming it into a captivating move, Campbell paved the way for a unique dance form that continues to inspire and entertain audiences worldwide. Whether you're a seasoned Locker or a beginner, mastering the Lock is essential to truly embodying the spirit and energy of this dynamic dance style.
